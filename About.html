Q&A session (Answers):
    I didn't start my journey as a programmer with a clear plan. At first, it was simply curiosity — wanting to understand what happens behind the screen. Gradually, that curiosity turned into practice, and practice slowly became a habit.
    I enjoy building things from scratch and embracing the challenges that come with debugging and failure. During this process, I met Xiaoyun Li, Xinshuo Ji, Wei Li, Wangzi Chen, Bo Qu, and Chang Wei. To me, they have been more than classmates or collaborators; they are friends, even like family. Programming, for me, is more than writing code. It's a way to train my thinking, solve problems, and cultivate patience.
      As for this blog, the idea started simply — I wanted a space to document my learning, my thought process, and my growth. It doesn't need to be perfect; it just needs to be honest and allow me to move forward consistently. Yet for a long time, I didn't have a clear direction. Everything changed during my internship at Boeing, where I met Eric, a returnee from studying abroad. He gave me unprecedented inspiration. During our time together, he helped me strengthen the areas I was weak in, shared experiences and ways of thinking that books never teach, and showed me the depth of his own abilities. His influence prompted me to reconsider what I wanted to create and leave behind.
    Finally, I sincerely thank everyone who has helped me along the way, and my parents for their unwavering support. Thank you all for allowing me to continue moving forward on this path.
